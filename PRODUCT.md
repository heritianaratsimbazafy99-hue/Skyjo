# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Skyjo Score Arena is used by friends or family playing Skyjo around a physical table. One screen acts as the shared scoreboard and atmosphere display, while phones can join as score controllers through a QR code. Users need fast score entry, clear ranking changes, visible rules reminders, and lively feedback that keeps the game feeling social.

## Product Purpose

The product replaces paper scorekeeping with a synchronized visual arena: add players, track rounds, apply official scoring rules, optionally activate Deck Chaos, follow live rankings, and celebrate the end of the game. Success means the table can understand the current state at a glance and every score action feels immediate, playful, and trustworthy.

## Brand Personality

Bright, kinetic, playful. The interface should feel like a modern board-game companion: colorful and animated, but still legible during a real game where people glance quickly between cards, phones, and the shared screen.

## Anti-references

Avoid the current dark, heavy, static atmosphere. Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, dull gray panels, decorative motion that slows the game, overdone glass effects, emoji-as-icons, identical card grids, and any visual treatment that makes scoring feel less readable.

## Design Principles

1. Make the shared screen feel alive without making users wait.
2. Keep score entry and ranking comprehension faster than paper.
3. Use color to clarify player identity, status, tension, and celebration.
4. Treat mobile and desktop as one synchronized table experience.
5. Preserve official rules and edge cases in plain, confidence-building language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive controls. All actions must remain keyboard reachable, form fields must keep visible labels, color must not be the only status indicator, touch targets should be at least 44px, and all animation needs a reduced-motion path.
